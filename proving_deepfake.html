Detecting Deepfakes

There are ways to verify or strongly challenge whether audio, video, or images are AI-generated. No single test is perfect, but a layered approach—provenance + watermark checks + forensic analysis + OSINT/context—does work in practice.

No single tool or test can always confirm whether a video, image, or audio clip is AI-generated.

Using several methods listed below together greatly increases accuracy.

Provenance tools like Adobe’s Content Credentials and Truepic’s C2PA signatures can verify if media was captured authentically.

Watermarking systems such as Google’s SynthID and Meta’s AudioSeal/VideoSeal help identify AI-originated content when supported.

Forensic detectors like Reality Defender, Sensity AI, and Pindrop specialize in scanning for digital artifacts and patterns left behind by generative models.

OSINT resources like the Bellingcat Toolkit and Amnesty’s YouTube DataViewer provide context checks through reverse search and metadata analysis.

By layering provenance, watermarking, forensic detection, and open-source verification, victims and investigators can build a strong case to prove manipulation and push for takedowns more effectively.
